# New Zealand InSAR Papers

!!! warning "Under review"
    This page was assembled by Claude (Anthropic) drawing on Dani Lindsay's thesis work, research notes, and guidance. It has not yet been through a final review — if you see this notice, treat the content as a useful starting point but verify anything you plan to cite or act on.

---

InSAR and geodesy papers relevant to New Zealand — covering active faults, the Hikurangi subduction zone, Taupo Volcanic Zone, landslides, and urban subsidence. One-sentence takeaways are Dani's interpretations.

*Maintained by Danielle Lindsay.*

---

| | Reference | Link | Takeaway |
|---|-----------|------|----------|
| | Langridge, R.M., et al. (2016). The New Zealand Active Faults Database. *New Zealand Journal of Geology and Geophysics*, 59, 86–96. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/00288306.2015.1112818) | The reference catalogue for active fault geometry in NZ — the starting point for any InSAR study targeting fault creep or coseismic deformation. |
| | Hamling, I.J., Wright, T.J., Hreinsdóttir, S., & Wallace, L.M. (2022). A snapshot of New Zealand's dynamic deformation field from Envisat InSAR and GNSS observations between 2003 and 2011. *Geophysical Research Letters*, 49(2), e2021GL096465. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2021GL096465) | Baseline interseismic velocity field for NZ from Envisat C-band — a key reference for what the background deformation field looks like before starting any new study. |
| | Wallace, L.M. (2020). Slow slip events in New Zealand. *Annual Review of Earth and Planetary Sciences*, 48(1), 175–203. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-earth-071719-055104) | Comprehensive review of Hikurangi slow slip — essential background for anyone working on the subduction zone or on transient signals in NZ InSAR data. |
| | Hamling, I.J. (2021). InSAR observations over the Taupō Volcanic Zone's cone volcanoes: insights and challenges from the New Zealand volcano supersite. *New Zealand Journal of Geology and Geophysics*, 64(2–3), 347–357. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/00288306.2020.1779848) | Honest account of what InSAR can and cannot detect across NZ's arc volcanoes — including the decorrelation and atmospheric noise challenges specific to this environment. |
| | Haghshenas Haghighi, M., & Motagh, M. (2016). Assessment of ground surface displacement in Taihape landslide, New Zealand, with C- and X-band SAR interferometry. *New Zealand Journal of Geology and Geophysics*, 59(1), 136–146. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/00288306.2015.1127824) | C- and X-band comparison for a slow NZ landslide — useful context on what different wavelengths can resolve at typical NZ landslide velocities. |
| | Cao, Y., Hamling, I., Massey, C., & Upton, P. (2023). Slow-moving landslides triggered by the 2016 Mw 7.8 Kaikōura earthquake, New Zealand: a new InSAR phase-gradient based time-series approach. *Geophysical Research Letters*, 50(4), e2022GL102064. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2022GL102064) | Phase-gradient time series approach for detecting co- and post-seismic landslide accelerations across the Kaikōura rupture zone — methodologically useful for triggered-slip studies. |
| | Kearse, J., Stern, T., Hamling, I., Lamb, S., & Hreinsdóttir, S. (2025). InSAR measurement of vertical land motion in New Zealand cities, and implications for sea-level rise projections. *New Zealand Journal of Geology and Geophysics*, 68(4), 794–815. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/00288306.2024.2354076) | Sentinel-1 vertical land motion in NZ urban centres — shows both subsidence (Wellington, Christchurch) and uplift signals, directly relevant to sea-level hazard assessment. |
| | McGrath, J.D., Elliott, J.R., Watson, A.R., Wright, T.J., Piazolo, S., & Hamling, I.J. (2025). Linking geodetically resolved uplift to long-term orogenic exhumation in the Southern Alps, New Zealand. *Journal of Geophysical Research: Solid Earth*, 130(11), e2024JB030625. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2024JB030625) | Connects InSAR-measured rock uplift across the Southern Alps to million-year exhumation rates — a good example of InSAR bridging geodetic and geological timescales. |
| | Bruce, Z.R.V. (2022). Version 1 of the New Zealand landslide fatalities database, 1760–2020. GNS Science Report 2021/38. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.21420/SW43-TJ28) | Historical record of NZ landslide fatalities — useful context for framing the hazard significance of slow-moving landslide monitoring. |
| | Lindsay, D., Bürgmann, R., Materna, K., & Fielding, E.J. (*in press*). Nine-year L-band InSAR time series of tectonic and non-tectonic surface deformation in northern California. *Journal of Geophysical Research: Solid Earth*. | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2025JB032718) | ALOS-2 ScanSAR time series methodology — ionospheric correction, split-spectrum, multi-track velocity fields. Northern California data, but the methods are directly applicable to NZ L-band work. |
