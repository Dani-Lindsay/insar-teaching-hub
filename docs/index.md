# InSAR Teaching Hub

A curated collection of learning resources, workflows, and literature for students getting started with **Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR)**.

This hub is aimed at masters-level students with a geology or earth science background who have likely encountered InSAR in the literature but have not yet processed or interpreted data themselves.

Resources here point to the best existing tutorials, tools, and papers — the goal is to save you time finding them and give you a structured pathway through them.

## Where to start

New to InSAR? Follow this pathway:

1. **[History of InSAR](history.md)** — understand where the technique came from and the key papers that shaped it
2. **[Getting Started](getting-started.md)** — what InSAR measures, how it works, and the key concepts you need before touching data
3. **[Single Interferogram](workflows/single-interferogram.md)** — order and explore your first interferogram using ASF OnDemand
4. **[Time Series](workflows/hyp3-mintpy.md)** — build a displacement time series with HyP3 products and MintPy
